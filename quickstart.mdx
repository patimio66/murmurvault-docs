This guide takes you from a fresh account to your first stored secret. It should take about five minutes.

## Prerequisites

- A modern browser with [WebAuthn](https://webauthn.guide/) support (current Chrome, Safari, Firefox, or Edge).
- An authenticator for passkeys — a platform authenticator (Touch ID, Windows Hello, Android) or a security key.
- Access to your email, used to verify your address and to release top-tier secrets.

<Note>
  murmurvault is served over HTTPS, which passkeys require. You don't need to configure anything for this.
</Note>

## Get started

<Steps>
  <Step title="Create your account">
    Register with your name, email, and a password, then confirm your email from the verification link.

    This password protects your **account** (signing in). It is separate from the **vault passphrase** you set next, which protects your **secrets**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Create a vault">
    Open the vault and choose **Create vault**. Set a vault passphrase.

    Your passphrase derives the key that unlocks your secrets. The strength meter helps you pick something strong. Choose carefully — it cannot be reset or recovered.

    <Warning>
      No one can recover your passphrase, including the server operator. Store it somewhere safe before you continue.
    </Warning>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add a passkey (recommended)">
    In **Settings → Vault**, link a passkey to the vault. A passkey lets you unlock without typing your passphrase and is required for top-tier secrets.

    See [Passkeys](/vault/passkeys) for details.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Store your first secret">
    Back in the vault, choose **Add secret**, pick a type (KV, Login, Recovery Codes, or Secure Note), name it, and fill in the fields. Save.

    The secret is encrypted in your browser before it is sent. The server stores only ciphertext.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Lock and unlock">
    Lock the vault, then unlock it again with your passphrase or passkey to confirm your setup works.

    The vault also auto-locks after a period of inactivity (10 minutes by default). See [Unlocking](/vault/unlocking).
  </Step>
</Steps>
