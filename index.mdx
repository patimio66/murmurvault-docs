murmurvault is an end-to-end encrypted secret vault. You store API keys, logins, recovery codes, and notes; the server only ever holds ciphertext. Encryption and decryption happen in your browser, so your plaintext, passphrase, and keys never leave your device.

A full server breach exposes nothing but encrypted blobs and key-derivation parameters.

## What you can store

| Type | Fields |
|------|--------|
| KV | value, notes |
| Login | username, password, URL, notes |
| Recovery Codes | codes (one per line) |
| Secure Note | note |

Every secret also has a name. The name, type, and field values are all encrypted — the server cannot read them or group by them.

## Two tiers of secrets

Each secret is filed in one of two tiers:

- **Soft** — unlocked with your passphrase or any linked passkey.
- **Top** — additionally gated behind a passkey tap and a one-time code emailed to you.

See [Secret tiers](/concepts/secret-tiers) for how the two differ and when to use each.

## How your data is protected

- A client-generated **Master Key** encrypts all metadata and soft secrets.
- A separate **Top Key**, held only by passkeys, encrypts top-tier values.
- Keys are wrapped by your passphrase (Argon2id) and your passkeys (WebAuthn PRF).
- The cipher is XChaCha20-Poly1305 via libsodium.

The full design is described in [Security model](/concepts/security-model).

<Warning>
  There is no account recovery. Your passphrase and passkeys are the only way in. Lose all of them and your data is gone for good — by design.
</Warning>
