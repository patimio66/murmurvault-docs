---
title: "Unlocking & auto-lock"
description: "How to unlock a vault, how it stays unlocked, and when it locks itself."
---

A vault is locked until you unlock it. Unlocking loads the [Master Key](/concepts/security-model#keys) into memory so soft secrets can be decrypted. The Top Key is never loaded during a normal unlock — see [Top-tier secrets](/vault/top-secrets).

## Unlock a vault

Unlock with either:

- **Your passphrase** — the passphrase you set for this vault.
- **Any linked passkey** — tap or authenticate with a passkey you've linked to the vault.

Once unlocked, soft secrets decrypt as you open them, and the search palette can find secrets by name.

## Staying unlocked

To avoid re-entering credentials on every page, murmurvault caches the Master Key locally:

- Wrapped with AES-GCM under a non-extractable WebCrypto key.
- Stored in IndexedDB with a rolling expiry.

This means the unlocked state **survives page navigation and reloads** until the vault auto-locks. The Top Key is never cached.

## Auto-lock

Each vault locks itself after a period of inactivity.

- **Default:** 10 minutes.
- **Configurable per vault** in [Settings → Vault](/settings/vault).

When the vault locks:

- The cached Master Key is cleared.
- Any revealed top-secret plaintext held in the page is discarded.
- You must unlock again to read secrets.

## Lock manually

Lock the vault yourself at any time — for example before stepping away from a shared machine. Locking immediately clears the in-memory and cached keys.

<Tip>
  On a shared or borrowed device, lock the vault and sign out when you're done. You can also end other browser sessions from [Settings → Authentication](/settings/authentication).
</Tip>
