A vault is an encrypted container for your secrets, protected by its own passphrase and passkeys. You can keep several vaults — for example, one for personal credentials and one for work.

## Create a vault

1. Open the vault area. If you have none yet, you land on the setup screen.
2. Choose **Create vault** and give it a name (optional) and a passphrase.
3. Save. The vault's keys are generated in your browser and wrapped by your passphrase.

A vault without a name is shown as `Vault {id}` in the sidebar.

<Warning>
  The passphrase you set here protects this vault's secrets and cannot be recovered. Record it safely before adding secrets.
</Warning>

## Multiple vaults

You can create up to **50 vaults** per account. Each has independent keys, a passphrase, passkeys, and auto-lock settings.

- Vaults appear in the sidebar in their saved order.
- Drag to **reorder** them; the order is saved per account.
- The first vault by position is your **default** — where you land when you open the app.

When you reach the limit, the **Add vault** action is disabled and further creation is rejected by the server.

## Switch and filter

- Select a vault in the sidebar to open it.
- Within a vault, filter the secret list by type (KV, Login, Recovery Codes, Secure Note).
- Use the search palette to find a secret quickly. See [Manage secrets](/vault/secrets#search).

## Delete a vault

Deleting a vault permanently destroys it and every secret inside.

1. Open the vault's settings.
2. Choose to delete, and **type the vault's confirmation name** (its display name in kebab-case) to confirm.
3. Re-confirm your **account password** if prompted.

<Warning>
  Deletion is immediate and irreversible. There is no trash and no recovery. Export or move anything you need first.
</Warning>

Because deletion can lock you out of your own data, murmurvault requires a recent password confirmation (a 15-minute window, much shorter than ordinary actions) before it proceeds.
