---
title: "Vault settings"
description: "Change a vault's passphrase, auto-lock timeout, and top-secret passkeys."
---

**Settings → Vault** controls the protection settings for a vault. Opening it requires a recent account-password confirmation.

## Passphrase

Change the vault passphrase. Because the passphrase wraps the [Master Key](/concepts/security-model#keys), changing it re-wraps that key under the new passphrase.

<Warning>
  Choose carefully. The passphrase cannot be recovered or reset — if you forget it and have no linked passkey, the vault is unrecoverable.
</Warning>

Passphrase changes require a recent password confirmation (a short 15-minute window) and are rate-limited, because this action can lock you out of your own secrets.

## Auto-lock

Set how long the vault stays unlocked while idle before it locks itself.

- **Default:** 10 minutes.
- Applies per vault.

When the timeout elapses, cached keys and any revealed top-secret plaintext are cleared. See [Unlocking & auto-lock](/vault/unlocking#auto-lock).

## Top-secret passkeys

Grant or manage which passkeys hold the [Top Key](/concepts/security-model#keys):

- Granting the Top Key to a passkey **enables the top tier** for this vault.
- Top secrets can then be created and revealed with that passkey.

<Warning>
  murmurvault blocks removing the **last** passkey that holds the Top Key while top secrets exist — otherwise those secrets would become permanently unreadable. Keep more than one Top-Key passkey.
</Warning>

See [Top-tier secrets](/vault/top-secrets) and [Passkeys](/vault/passkeys).

## Delete the vault

You can also delete the vault from its settings. Deletion is immediate and irreversible, and asks you to type the vault's confirmation name. See [Vaults → Delete a vault](/vault/vaults#delete-a-vault).
