**Settings → Authentication** controls how you sign in to your account. These protections are separate from your vault passphrase and vault passkeys, which protect your secrets.

<Note>
  Opening this page requires a recent password confirmation. murmurvault may ask you to re-enter your account password before showing it.
</Note>

## Password

Change your account password from this page. Password updates are rate-limited to protect against abuse.

This is your **account** password (sign-in). It is not the **vault passphrase** that unlocks your secrets — changing one does not affect the other.

## Two-factor authentication (TOTP)

Add time-based one-time password (TOTP) two-factor authentication:

1. Start 2FA setup and scan the QR code with an authenticator app.
2. Confirm with a generated code to enable it.
3. Store the **recovery codes** somewhere safe — they let you sign in if you lose your authenticator.

Once enabled, sign-in asks for a code from your authenticator app (or a recovery code).

## Authentication passkeys

Register passkeys to sign in to your account without a password.

<Warning>
  Passkey registration requires HTTPS. It fails on plain `http://`.
</Warning>

These **authentication passkeys** are distinct from the **vault passkeys** that unlock a vault and hold the Top Key. See [Passkeys](/vault/passkeys) for vault passkeys.

## Browser sessions

Review where you're signed in and **sign out other sessions**. This is the first thing to do after using a shared or borrowed device.

Ending other sessions re-asserts your password in the request, so a confirmation from earlier is not enough on its own — protecting you when a confirmation window is still open on a machine you no longer control.
