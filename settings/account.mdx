---
title: "Account deletion"
description: "Delete your account through a delayed, reversible freeze-then-erase flow."
---

Deleting your account is a delayed, reversible process: murmurvault freezes the account first and erases it later, giving you a window to change your mind.

## How it works

<Steps>
  <Step title="Request deletion">
    Start deletion from [Settings → Profile](/settings/profile). This schedules erasure for a fixed grace period in the future and **freezes** the account immediately.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Grace period">
    The account stays frozen for **7 days**. During this time it is reachable only to confirm or cancel the deletion.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Reminder">
    A reminder email is sent **3 days** before the scheduled erasure, so the deletion never happens without warning.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Erasure">
    If you do nothing, the account and all its vaults and secrets are permanently deleted on the scheduled date.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Cancel a pending deletion

To stop the process during the grace period, sign back in and **cancel** the deletion from the pending-deletion screen. The freeze lifts and your account returns to normal.

<Note>
  You can reach and cancel the freeze even if your email isn't verified — a frozen account must always be able to escape the freeze.
</Note>

## After erasure

<Warning>
  Once the grace period elapses and erasure runs, the account, its vaults, and every secret are gone permanently. There is no recovery after this point.
</Warning>
